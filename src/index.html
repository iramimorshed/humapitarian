of a community. Unfortunately, many nations are currently and have been experiencing humanitarian crises, most receiving little to no media coverage.

Humapitarian was designed to summarize such events in a concise and digestible format, thus raising awareness and urging others to further educate themselves and take action.

PALESTINE

Disclaimer: The Israeli-Palestinian conflict is a century-old, and as a result, it is difficult to summarize such persistent tensions between the two groups within a few lines. I have summarized the current humanitarian crises occurring in Palestine, but I embolden readers to educate themselves about the context in which these crises have been unfolding.

The 11-Day War

Dubbed the 11-Day War, fighting ignited on May 10, 2021 between the Palestinan Hamas militants in Gaza and Israeli forces. This was triggered by days of dispute between violent police forces and Palestinian civilians at the holy Al-Aqsa Mosque. In addition, tensions became more inflamed due to Jewish settlers threatening to evict dozens of Palestinian families.

Israeli Defense Forces report 3,750 rockets were fired by the Hamas militants into Israel, killing 12 people, including a 5-year-old boy and 16-year-old girl.In contrast, Israel’s air strikes killed 230 Palestinians—including 65 children and 39 women—in densely-populated areas of Gaza. After eleven days and global pressure, Israel announced a ceasefire on May 21st. NPR, ABC News

Prior to the ceasefire, around 230 buildings in Gaza, consisting of 991+ housing and commercial units, were destroyed by aerial assaults. 72,000+ people were displaced in the week leading up to the ceasefire. The bombardment hindered an already dwindling electricity supply in Gaza, only coming on for a few hours daily and thus impacting electricity-reliant medical devices. “That is taxing a health care system already strained by the pandemic,” reports Jen Kirby, a Vox reporter. Vox

The Gaza Strip

The Gaza Strip, named after the 140 square miles strip of land where 2 million Palestinians live, is nicknamed the world’s largest open-air prison (NRC). Since 2007, Israel has continued its illegal blockade of the Gaza Strip, restricting entry of goods and movement of people in and out of the area (Vox). The 14-year blockade, the pandemic, and fighting have stretched the healthcare system thin in Gaza. The World Health Organization reports the damage of 19 health facilities in the Gaza Strip.

YEMEN

Disclaimer: The Yemen Civil War erupted in 2014, but has escalated to include many other nations such as Iran, Saudi Arabia, the United States just to name a few. Because this war is a complex military conflict involving several key players, I have summarized the current humanitarian crisis that emerged as a consequence of the Yemen war. However, I urge readers to educate themselves about the origins of the war and who exactly is pitted against each other.

The World's Worst Humanitarian Crisis

According to the United Nations, the humanitarian crisis in Yemen is the worst in the world. Yemen has a poverty rate exceeding 50%, and 80% of the population (~23.2 million) is reliant on humanitarian assistance. About 13 million people are facing starvation. In addition to crisis-level food insecurity, disease continues to run rampant. In 2019, a cholera outbreak was recorded as the second-worst outbreak in international history, infecting about 700,000 people (Council on Foreign Relations). “UNICEF estimates that a child dies every ten minutes in Yemen of preventable diseases, about 50,000 and growing each year” (Center for Disaster Philanthropy ).

The Yemen Civil War

The war has killed about 130,000 people (as of March 2021) of which 12,000+ are civilians (Al-Jazeera News). The Houthi rebels are backed primarily by Iran, and Saudia Arabia organized a coalition in opposition. Nations such as the UAE, United States, and the UK have backed the Saudi-led coalition. The United States is Saudia Arabia’s largest arms supplier, and its weapons have caused numerous civilian deaths in the coalition-led airstrikes. Council on Foreign Relations

The Yemen Data Project reports the Saudi-led coalition has carried out between 20,624 to 58,487 airstrikes since March of 2015. Nearly a third of the aerial assaults hit civilian-populated areas such as homes, hospitals, schools, markets, mosques (Human Rights Watch). 4 million+ people remain internally displaced within Yemen, 73% of whom are women and children (Center for Disaster Philanthropy).

SYRIA

Disclaimer: The current humanitarian crisis in Syria has persisted since the start of the Syrian Civil War on March 15, 2011. There are many parties involved in the military conflict, including numerous rebel groups, the Syrian government, and foreign nations such as Russia and Turkey. Please take my research with a grain of salt, and I encourage readers to educate themselves about the details of this war not covered by the following summary.

A Humanitarian Disaster

The Syrian Civil War has created the largest refugee crisis at this time, with an estimated 13.5 million Syrians (more than 50% of the county's population) forcibly displaced. Those remaining in Syria are considered internally displaced, since most have lost their homes. World Vision

13 million+ people desperately need humanitarian aid. An additional 1 million have been forced into poverty since the COVID-19 pandemic started. The UN Refugee Agency

Peaceful pro-democracy protests against the Syrian government have escalated into a decade-long civil war, leaving 380,000+ people dead (as of December 2020) including 116,911+ civilians. The death toll does not take into account the 205,000+ missing or presumed dead people with 80,000 civilians included in this amount. BBC News

More than 80% of the population live in extreme poverty, living off of $1.90 per day. Due to the conflict, 8 million+ people lack access to safe drinkable water and infrastructure has also collapsed with only 53% of hospitals fully functional. World Vision

A Refugee Crisis

Syrian refugees have sought asylum in 130+ nations, but most of those forcibly displaced live in neighboring nations. Turkey hosts the largest population of Syrians (about 3.6 million) and in Lebanon,one in four residents is a Syrian refugee. The UN Refugee Agency

Destruction Caused By The War

Physicians for Human Rights reports 595 attacks on 350 distinct medical facilities (as of March 2020), regardless of their protected status. These attacks caused the deaths of 900+ medical employees. BBC News

Much of the country has been reduced to ruin due to a decade's worth of fighting. According to UN's satellite analysis, 35,000+ structures were either destroyed or damaged in Aleppo (a city) alone. BBC News

War Crimes

War crime investigators for the UN accused all involved parties of severe violations. They report Syrian civilians have been subject to chemical warfare, sieges to purposefully starve the population, restriction of humanitarian aid, constant aerial bombardments on densely-populated areas. BBC News

Parties Involved in War

After the country exploded into war, foreign nations and extremist groups such as Al-Qaeda and the Islamic State further intensified the chaos. The Syrian government is backed by Russia and Iran, while the opposition rebel forces are supported by Turkey, US, UK, France, and several Gulf Arab states. Saudi Arabia and Israel have also been involved. BBC News
